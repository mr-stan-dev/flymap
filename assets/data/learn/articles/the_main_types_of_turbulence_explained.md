# The Main Types of Turbulence Explained

Not all turbulence comes from the same source.

Pilots and meteorologists think about different kinds of turbulence depending on what is causing the uneven air.

---

## 🌤 1. Convective Turbulence

This happens when warm air rises and cooler air sinks.

It is common around:

- sunny afternoons
- towering clouds
- thunderstorms

The air can move strongly up and down in these conditions.

---

## ⛰ 2. Mechanical or Mountain-Related Turbulence

When wind flows over hills, buildings, or mountains, it can become disturbed.

That creates rougher air downwind of the obstacle.

Mountain waves are a well-known example.

---

## 🌬 3. Clear-Air Turbulence

This type happens in clear skies, often near strong wind changes or jet streams.

It can be harder to spot visually because there may be no cloud warning.

---

## ✈️ 4. Wake Turbulence

Aircraft can also create turbulence for each other.

Large aircraft leave behind swirling air, which is why spacing rules are important during takeoff and landing.

---

## ✨ What It Means

Turbulence is one broad feeling, but it can come from several different atmospheric or operational causes.

That is why pilots use different strategies depending on the situation.

---

## 💡 Simple Way to Think About It

Different turbulence types are like:
> different kinds of rough roads in the sky...  
> each caused by something different.

---

## 🟢 Quick Fact

Two flights on the same day can experience turbulence for completely different reasons depending on location and weather.

---

Understanding the main types of turbulence helps explain why bumpy air does not always look or behave the same.
