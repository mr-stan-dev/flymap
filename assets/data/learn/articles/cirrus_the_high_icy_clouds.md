# Cirrus: The High Icy Clouds

High above most clouds, you might see thin, delicate streaks stretching across the sky.  
These are **cirrus clouds** — the highest and lightest clouds you’ll encounter.

---

## ☁️ 1. Clouds at Extreme Altitude

Cirrus clouds form very high in the atmosphere:

👉 typically above **6–10 km**

At this height:
- the air is extremely cold
- water freezes instantly

---

## ❄️ 2. Made of Ice, Not Water

Unlike most clouds, cirrus clouds are made of:

- tiny ice crystals

These crystals:
- reflect sunlight
- create soft, glowing patterns

---

## 🌬 3. Why They Look So Thin

At high altitude:

- the air is very thin
- there’s less moisture

👉 So cirrus clouds appear:
- wispy
- feather-like
- stretched across the sky

---

## 🌤 4. A Sign of Change

Cirrus clouds often appear before weather changes.

They can indicate:
- an approaching warm front
- changing atmospheric conditions

👉 They are like early signals in the sky.

---

## ✈️ 5. What You See from a Plane

At cruising altitude, planes may:

- fly near cirrus clouds
- pass through thin layers of them

From the window, they look:
- soft and delicate
- almost like brush strokes in the sky

---

## ✨ What It Feels Like

Cirrus clouds give the sky a:
- calm
- elegant
- almost artistic appearance

---

## 💡 Simple Way to Think About It

Cirrus clouds are like:
> icy feathers drifting at the top of the atmosphere.

---

## 🟢 Quick Fact

Because they are made of ice crystals, cirrus clouds can create optical effects like halos around the Sun.

---

Cirrus clouds may look light and fragile —  
but they exist in some of the coldest, highest parts of our atmosphere.
